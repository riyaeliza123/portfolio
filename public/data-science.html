Overview

Currently applying data science to marine environmental monitoring, I've develop automated data pipelines, analytics tools, and machine learning models used by conservation research teams and partner agencies. My work includes models for monitoring, large-scale data processing, and dashboards that make complex data easier to understand and act on. I believe that robust data pipelines can support robust research and make high-quality data work possible in underrepresented fields.

Most of my analytics work supports environmental and conservation research, where data is often fragmented, large-scale, and time-sensitive. I aim to build systems that are reliable, transparent, and useful for both technical and non-technical users.

Projects and Impact

1. Bottlenecks Survival Project: Data Overview Dashboard

Feb 2025 – Sep 2025

Problem

Research data collected since 2019 was spread across multiple tables and programs, making it difficult for scientists to track tagging progress, monitor work across watersheds, and understand survival patterns over time. Teams needed a single place to view and explore data across detection records, tagging information, locations, and pilot programs.

Data

  • 400,000+ PIT-tagged salmon records
  • Detection data, tagging metadata, and biological attributes
  • Location and watershed information
  • Data from pilot programs (microtroll work, Avid Anglers)
  • Infrastructure details at monitoring sites
  • Outmigration and return season data from 2020 onward

Solution & Impact

  • Built a cross-filterable dashboard, consolidating research data from 2019 onward into one place
  • Enabled tracking of tagging counts by location and watershed, hatchery vs. wild fish, survival and bycatch trends, and seasonal movement patterns
  • Designed the interface so researchers can explore data without writing SQL or interacting with backend systems
  • Replaced manual querying and spreadsheet-based reporting with an interactive, self-serve tool
  • Reduced reporting and data preparation time by ~90%
  • Used internally by Pacific Salmon Foundation and BCCF, and shared with partner organizations and First Nations partners to support program tracking and planning

Focus

Data integration, Analytics, Visualization

Tools

Apache Superset, SQL, Python, CSS

2. Pinniped Census Data Pipeline (On-shore Cameras & Drone Imagery)

Sep 2025 – Dec 2025

Problem

Pinniped census data was collected through motion-activated on-shore cameras and drone flights, but counts were done manually. This required researchers to sift through large volumes of images, slowing down reporting and making census updates time-intensive. The team needed a faster, more consistent way to track pinniped presence across locations to support salmon research.

Data

  • Motion-triggered on-shore camera images
  • Drone imagery collected across multiple coastal locations
  • Image data stored across local drives and OneDrive
  • Census data recorded in ODK databases

Solution

  • Built an end-to-end pipeline that ingests on-shore and drone imagery, runs automated detection, and returns annotated images with per-site pinniped counts
  • Developed a simple Streamlit app allowing users to upload images or submit OneDrive links, with outputs ready for download or direct submission to the ODK database
  • Replaced manual image review and data entry, reducing census effort from ~1 week per site to under 10 minutes
  • Deployed as a public-facing tool and actively used by the PSF team for ongoing pinniped census work

Focus

Data pipelines, workflow automation, usability, and reducing manual effort

Tools

Python, Streamlit, YOLOv11, cloud storage (S3), ODK
